# Music Energy Profiles

Energy in music is a measure of perceived intensity and activity level, scored from 0.0 (completely calm) to 1.0 (maximally intense). It combines loudness, tempo, rhythmic density, and dynamic range into a single continuous value. Understanding energy levels helps match music to listening contexts — the right energy level is as important as the right genre or mood.

---

## Low Energy (0.0 – 0.4)

Low energy music is quiet, unhurried, and undemanding. It prioritizes texture and atmosphere over rhythm and drive. These tracks tend to have low loudness, slow to moderate tempos (below ~90 BPM), and a high degree of acousticness or sonic sparseness.

**Songs in this range:**
- Adagio in Grey (0.22) — classical/melancholic, the most subdued track in the catalog
- Spacewalk Thoughts (0.28) — ambient/chill
- Campfire Lullaby (0.29) — folk/peaceful
- Library Rain (0.35) — lofi/chill
- Coffee Shop Stories (0.37) — jazz/relaxed
- Focus Flow (0.40) — lofi/focused

**Listening contexts for low energy music:** Sleep preparation, meditation, deep reading, background listening during delicate work, emotional processing, recovery from stress, and any situation where the listener wants to lower arousal rather than sustain or increase it.

Low energy music does not mean low quality or low engagement — classical and jazz at low energy can be profoundly complex. Energy describes physical intensity, not emotional or intellectual depth.

---

## Medium Energy (0.4 – 0.7)

Medium energy music has rhythm and groove but doesn't push the listener. It creates forward motion without urgency — the sweet spot for most everyday listening. These tracks have moderate loudness, tempos typically between 80-110 BPM, and a balanced mix of acoustic and produced elements.

**Songs in this range:**
- Midnight Coding (0.42) — lofi/chill
- Dirt Road Hymn (0.43) — country/nostalgic
- River Bottom Blues (0.47) — blues/soulful
- Velvet Hours (0.54) — r&b/romantic
- Neon Fog (0.53) — electronic/dreamy
- Island Time (0.60) — reggae/uplifting

**Listening contexts for medium energy music:** Working while listening, casual social settings, meals, commuting at a relaxed pace, and any context where the listener wants musical companionship without being driven or overwhelmed. Medium energy is the most versatile zone — it rarely feels out of place.

---

## High Energy (0.7 – 1.0)

High energy music is intense, propulsive, and physically engaging. It features high loudness, fast tempos (100+ BPM, often 130+), heavy rhythmic drive, and production choices designed to create maximum stimulation. High energy songs dominate playlists for exercise, dancing, and high-focus sprints.

**Songs in this range:**
- Night Drive Loop (0.75) — synthwave/moody
- Rooftop Lights (0.76) — indie pop/happy
- Block Party Anthem (0.78) — hip-hop/confident
- Sunrise City (0.82) — pop/happy
- Gym Hero (0.93) — pop/intense
- Storm Runner (0.91) — rock/intense
- Overdrive (0.96) — edm/energetic
- Iron Curtain (0.97) — metal/aggressive

**Listening contexts for high energy music:** Physical exercise, dancing, commuting with urgency, emotional release, high-output creative sprints, and any context where the listener wants to feel energized, motivated, or physically activated.

**Important:** High energy covers a wide emotional range. Sunrise City (0.82, pop/happy) and Iron Curtain (0.97, metal/aggressive) are both high energy but create completely different emotional experiences. Energy level alone doesn't define the mood — genre and mood tags provide that additional dimension.

---

## Energy and Mood Interaction

Energy and mood are related but independent dimensions:

- **High energy + happy** = celebratory pop, upbeat indie — "Sunrise City," "Rooftop Lights"
- **High energy + intense** = driving rock, aggressive metal — "Storm Runner," "Iron Curtain"
- **High energy + confident** = hip-hop anthems — "Block Party Anthem"
- **Low energy + peaceful** = folk, acoustic — "Campfire Lullaby"
- **Low energy + melancholic** = classical, introspective — "Adagio in Grey"
- **Medium energy + romantic** = R&B, smooth — "Velvet Hours"
- **Medium energy + dreamy** = electronic, hazy — "Neon Fog"

The best recommendations match both the target energy AND the mood — a high-energy but peaceful track is nearly impossible to find because the two signals conflict.
