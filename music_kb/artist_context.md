# Artist Context Knowledge Base

This document provides brief background on the artists featured in the VibeFinder catalog. Use this context to personalize explanations with artist-specific framing.

---

## Neon Echo

Neon Echo is a versatile electronic and pop producer whose catalog spans bright pop anthems and moody synthwave instrumentals. "Sunrise City" showcases their pop songwriting — high valence, catchy hooks, and polished 2020s production. "Night Drive Loop" reveals their darker side: synthwave atmosphere, slow-building tension, and cinematic texture. Neon Echo demonstrates how a single artist can serve completely different listener moods depending on the track.

## LoRoom

LoRoom specializes in lofi instrumental music built for focus and ambient listening. Their two catalog entries — "Midnight Coding" and "Focus Flow" — are both in the chill-to-focused mood range with low-to-moderate energy. LoRoom's production philosophy prioritizes warmth, analog imperfection, and consistency over surprise: they make music you can live inside for hours without it pulling you out of your flow.

## Voltline

Voltline is a rock band occupying the intense, high-energy end of the genre. "Storm Runner" is their catalog entry — 0.91 energy, 152 BPM, rock/intense. Voltline's sound draws from hard rock and alternative metal traditions: distorted guitars, driven drumming, and a sense of propulsion that rewards listeners seeking music with physical force. They are the go-to choice for intense-mood, high-energy rock needs.

## Paper Lanterns

Paper Lanterns makes soft lofi instrumentals with a particular gift for acoustic texture. "Library Rain" is their catalog entry — among the lowest energy tracks (0.35), chill mood, high acousticness (0.86). The name reflects their aesthetic: delicate, handcrafted, slightly melancholy, and warm. Paper Lanterns suits late-night studying or rain-watching more than any other artist in the catalog.

## Max Pulse

Max Pulse is a pop producer known for high-energy workout and motivational tracks. "Gym Hero" is their catalog entry — 0.93 energy, pop/intense, with the highest danceability in the pop category (0.88). Max Pulse's production is deliberately aggressive for a pop context: it sacrifices some warmth for drive. They are ideal for listeners who want pop's accessibility with genuinely intense energy.

## Orbit Bloom

Orbit Bloom creates ambient and atmospheric electronic music. "Spacewalk Thoughts" is their entry — 0.28 energy, ambient/chill, with the second-highest acousticness in the catalog (0.92). Orbit Bloom's music functions more as environmental design than traditional songs: it shapes the emotional atmosphere of a space without drawing attention to itself. It works best as meditative or background listening.

## Slow Stereo

Slow Stereo is a jazz ensemble focused on warm, conversational instrumental music. "Coffee Shop Stories" (0.37 energy, jazz/relaxed, released 2000) evokes the intimacy of live small-venue jazz. Slow Stereo's production retains the natural imperfections of acoustic performance — the room sound, the slight timing variations — that give jazz its spontaneous feeling.

## Indigo Parade

Indigo Parade is an indie pop act with a talent for bright, emotionally effortless songs. "Rooftop Lights" is their catalog entry — 0.76 energy, indie pop/happy, high valence (0.81). Indigo Parade occupies the space between mainstream pop and indie music: they have pop's emotional directness but produce with a more textured, personality-driven sound. Released in 2010, "Rooftop Lights" has the nostalgic quality of peak indie pop era production.

## Crowd Control

Crowd Control is a hip-hop act built around collective energy and confident self-expression. "Block Party Anthem" (0.78 energy, hip-hop/confident, 2020) reflects their name: it's designed for groups, for public spaces, for moments when you want music that feels communal and assertive. Crowd Control uses minimal but punchy production, letting the rhythmic momentum and lyrical delivery carry the song.

## Pulse Grid

Pulse Grid is an EDM producer specializing in maximum-energy club and festival tracks. "Overdrive" is their catalog entry — the highest energy track (0.96) with the highest danceability (0.95) and tempo (138 BPM). Pulse Grid's music is engineered rather than composed: every element exists to maximize physical response and dancefloor impact. They are the definitive choice for energetic mood and EDM genre needs.

## The Hollows

The Hollows is a country folk act rooted in acoustic storytelling traditions. "Dirt Road Hymn" (0.43 energy, country/nostalgic, 2010) evokes wide-open rural landscapes and the emotional sincerity of roots music. The Hollows uses fingerpicked guitar, fiddle undertones, and unhurried production that feels more like a family recording than a studio product. They serve nostalgic mood listeners who want authenticity over production polish.

## Vienna Strings

Vienna Strings is a classical chamber ensemble whose catalog entry is "Adagio in Grey" — the lowest-energy, most acoustically pure track in the catalog (acousticness 0.97, energy 0.22). The name signals their European classical tradition. Vienna Strings represents the catalog's most emotionally complex and technically demanding music — their melancholic, slow adagio rewards patient, attentive listening and suits contemplative or emotionally processing moods.

## Sable Rose

Sable Rose is an R&B artist specializing in intimate, romantic vocal music. "Velvet Hours" (0.54 energy, r&b/romantic, 2020) captures their aesthetic: warm production, smooth delivery, and lyrics tuned to close personal moments. Sable Rose's music feels tactile and sensory — it's designed for intimate settings rather than background listening. They represent the catalog's romantic mood needs and mid-energy R&B space.

## Cedar & Ash

Cedar & Ash is an acoustic folk duo whose name signals their sonic identity: natural materials, handmade quality, warmth. "Campfire Lullaby" (0.29 energy, folk/peaceful, 2010) is among the most stripped-down tracks in the catalog — high acousticness (0.91), gentle tempo (84 BPM), and a quality that sounds like music made for specific people in a specific place, not for mass production. Cedar & Ash is ideal for peaceful mood and low-energy listening needs.

## Wrath Engine

Wrath Engine is a metal band operating at the extreme end of the catalog's energy spectrum. "Iron Curtain" (0.97 energy, metal/aggressive, 2010) is the most sonically intense track available — 174 BPM, aggressive mood, minimal acousticness (0.04), and distorted low valence (0.28). Wrath Engine serves listeners who want cathartic emotional release through sonic intensity. They are unambiguously the choice for aggressive mood and maximum energy needs.

## Dusty Hale

Dusty Hale is a blues musician in the traditional American roots tradition. "River Bottom Blues" (0.47 energy, blues/soulful, 2000) captures the genre's essential quality: emotionally raw performance, expressive guitar work, and a production aesthetic that prioritizes authenticity over sophistication. Released in 2000, it has the warmth and grit of classic blues recordings. Dusty Hale serves soulful mood listeners who value musical honesty and historical depth.

## Coral Drift

Coral Drift is a reggae act whose music carries the genre's characteristic ease and warmth. "Island Time" (0.60 energy, reggae/uplifting, 2020) features the offbeat rhythm guitar "skank" essential to reggae, a bright lyrical character, and moderate-to-high energy that feels buoyant rather than driving. Coral Drift's music creates a physical sense of relaxed movement — it's music for warm environments, outdoor settings, and lifting moods without forcing the issue.

## Static Daydream

Static Daydream is an electronic producer specializing in dreamy, atmospheric synthesizer music. "Neon Fog" (0.53 energy, electronic/dreamy, 2020) exemplifies their approach: hazy production, blurred melodic edges, and a floating quality that sits between active listening and ambient experience. Static Daydream occupies the space between electronic music and ambient — structural enough to follow as a song, textural enough to drift into. They are ideal for dreamy mood needs and late-night introspective listening.
